Project Title: Mixed-Use Residential and Commercial Development

Client Overview:
The client is planning to develop a mid-scale mixed-use building in an urban area. The project should combine residential apartments with ground-floor commercial spaces. The target audience includes young professionals, small families, and retail business owners. The building must comply with local building regulations, fire safety standards, and environmental sustainability guidelines.

Site Information:
The site area is approximately 12,000 square feet. The allowable built-up area ratio is 2.5. The building height limit is 45 meters. The site is located on a corner plot with road access from two sides. Parking must be accommodated within the basement levels.

Residential Requirements:
The residential section should include:
- 10 studio apartments (approx. 450 sq ft each)
- 15 one-bedroom apartments (approx. 650 sq ft each)
- 10 two-bedroom apartments (approx. 950 sq ft each)

Each apartment must include:
- Living area
- Kitchen
- Bathroom
- Balcony where possible
Natural light and cross ventilation are important priorities.

Commercial Requirements:
The ground floor should include:
- 6 retail units (300–500 sq ft each)
- 1 café space with outdoor seating
- Common lobby area
The commercial area should have separate access from residential entries.

Amenities:
The project should include:
- Rooftop garden
- Small gymnasium
- Multipurpose hall
- Children's play area
- Landscaped entrance

Parking Requirements:
- Basement parking for at least 40 cars
- Separate entry and exit ramps
- Bicycle parking provisions
- Electric vehicle charging stations

Sustainability Considerations:
The building should aim for energy efficiency. Requirements include:
- Solar panels on rooftop
- Rainwater harvesting system
- Waste segregation area
- Energy-efficient lighting
- Provision for greywater recycling

Structural and Safety Requirements:
The structure must be earthquake-resistant as per zone regulations. Fire safety norms require:
- Fire exits on each floor
- Fire-rated staircases
- Sprinkler system
- Smoke detection system
- Emergency lighting

Accessibility:
The building must comply with accessibility standards:
- Ramps at entrances
- Elevator access to all floors
- Accessible toilets in commercial area

Future Expansion:
The design should allow for possible vertical expansion in the future if regulations permit.

The client expects a modern architectural aesthetic with clean lines, neutral color palettes, and glass façade elements while ensuring privacy for residential units. Cost efficiency and optimized space planning are critical factors. The overall goal is to maximize utility while maintaining aesthetic appeal and sustainability.
